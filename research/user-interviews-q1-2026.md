# User Research Summary — Q1 2026

**Method:** 1:1 interviews (45 min each)  
**n:** 12 participants across 8 accounts  
**Conducted by:** Design & PM  
**Date range:** Feb 3 – Mar 14, 2026

---

## Key Themes

### 1. Permissions is the #1 blocker to wider adoption

Every participant who manages a team of 5+ cited lack of permissions as a reason they hadn't rolled Launchpad out more broadly. The fear of accidental edits or premature launch visibility was a recurring concern.

> "We had an incident where someone archived a live launch by mistake. We've been manually auditing every week since." — Head of PMM, Series C SaaS company

### 2. Slack is the primary communication layer, not Launchpad

Most teams check Launchpad weekly, but they live in Slack. Participants wanted status updates pushed to Slack without needing to open Launchpad.

> "If it doesn't come to me in Slack, I'm not going to see it until standup." — Product Lead, e-commerce platform

### 3. Templates save time but feel rigid

The checklist template feature (v1.1) is widely adopted but users feel limited by the fixed structure. They want to reorder steps, add custom fields, and clone templates.

---

## Verbatim Highlights

- "I love the tool but my CEO can't see the Q3 roadmap launch — it's not announced yet."
- "The export is fine for reporting but I want a live dashboard my stakeholders can check."
- "Can I just star certain launches to see them first?"

---

## Recommendations

1. **Prioritize permissions** — unblocks expansion within existing accounts (land-and-expand signal)
2. **Build Slack integration next** — high frequency of use; low switching cost
3. **Invest in template flexibility** — reduce churn risk among power users
