# Launchpad — Q1 2026 User Research: One-Pager

**Prepared by:** Michael Mizener  
**Date:** May 19, 2026  
**Audience:** Internal stakeholders

---

## What we did

12 user interviews across 8 accounts, conducted February–March 2026. Participants ranged from Product Leads to Heads of PMM at Series A–C companies.

---

## What we heard

**Permissions is the #1 blocker to growth.**
Every user managing a team of 5+ said the lack of permissions had prevented them from rolling Launchpad out more broadly. Users are worried about accidental edits and sensitive launches being visible to the wrong people. One customer has been manually auditing their account every week after an incident where a live launch was archived by mistake.

**Users live in Slack, not Launchpad.**
Most teams check Launchpad once a week at most. They want status updates pushed to them in Slack rather than having to open the product. Without this, Launchpad risks becoming a tool that PMs maintain but stakeholders ignore.

**Templates are valuable but too rigid.**
The checklist template feature is widely adopted, but power users feel constrained. They want to reorder steps, add custom fields, and clone existing templates — none of which are currently possible.

---

## What we're doing about it

| Finding | Response | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Permissions blocking adoption | Role-based permissions (Viewer / Editor / Admin) | In progress — Q2 release |
| Slack dependency | Slack integration (status change notifications) | In progress — Q2 release |
| Template rigidity | Flexible templates (reorder, custom fields, clone) | Backlog |

---

## Bottom line

The two features shipping in Q2 — permissions and Slack integration — directly address the top two blockers our users named. Shipping both before Q3 renewals puts us in a strong position with the 6 named accounts that have flagged permissions as a requirement.

Template flexibility is the next area to invest in to reduce churn risk among our most engaged users.
